Under the Hood

Three agents. One structured analysis.

Each product goes through a three-stage analysis pulling from reviews, forums, and price data. You get a clear recommendation with the sources to back it up.

Agent 01: The Advocate

Builds the case for buying.

Pulls verified purchase reviews, editorial roundups, and spec sheets. Surfaces the product's performance data and most praised real-world use cases so you know what you'd be getting at its best.

  • → Top-rated review synthesis
  • → Spec benchmarking vs. category leaders
  • → Use-case fit scoring
Agent 02: The Skeptic

Looks for red flags and failure modes.

Mines Reddit threads, one-star reviews, and owner forums for failure modes that marketing buries. Focuses on what breaks over time, what owners regret, and what the spec sheet leaves out.

  • → Reddit & forum fail-point mining
  • → Long-term durability signals
  • → Common post-purchase regret patterns
Agent 03: The Economist

Checks the price and finds alternatives.

Analyzes 90 days of price history to tell you whether now is a good time to buy. Then searches the market for alternatives that offer better value per dollar, with a specific product recommendation.

  • → 90-day price history & trend analysis
  • → Value-per-dollar alternative ranking
  • → Buy now vs. wait recommendation
The Verdict Engine

The result: one clear recommendation.

After the analysis, a summary layer weighs each agent's findings, scores confidence, and returns one of three verdicts. Every report includes the full reasoning, source links, and a confidence score.

Verdict A
BUY IT

The product holds up under scrutiny. Specs, reviews, and price all check out for the use case.

Verdict B
SKIP IT

The Skeptic found enough failure modes to recommend passing. The report explains what went wrong.

Verdict C
BUY BETTER

The Economist found a competing product with better value. You get the specific alternative to consider.

Every verdict includes a full debate transcript, confidence score, and source links.

How this compares to existing research tools

It pulls from the same sources you'd check anyway. The difference is that it synthesizes those into a single structured recommendation, with the reasoning shown.

Review sites rely on affiliate revenue
Affiliate commissions mean "best of" lists often rank products by payout, not by how well they actually perform.
Reddit takes time to parse
Useful signal exists in threads and owner forums, but it's buried under noise and posts about discontinued hardware.
YouTube is entertainment, not analysis
Production value is high, but sponsored integrations are common and engagement tends to be optimized over accuracy.
General AI lacks live product data
Most AI assistants don't scrape current reviews, track price history, or run a structured analysis against a specific product.
